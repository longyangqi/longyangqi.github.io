Digging Into Self-Supervised Monocular Depth Estimation (ICCV19 Godard UCL)

Problem

  • Self-supervised monocular depth estimation. Training with monocular video, stereo pairs, or both.
  • Training phase: inputs: multi-frames(2). outputs: depth and relative pose. Testing phase: inputs: single image, outputs: depth
  • Challenges: (1) occlusion (2) moving object/stationary pixels

Scientific Hypothesis

  • Instead of complex architecture, simple model + design choices :arrow_right: superior results
  • Assumptions of self-supervised training (photometric error): appearance (brightness) and material properties (Lambertian) of objects, and a moving camera and a static scene

Related Works

  • supervised learning :arrow_right: semi-supervised (object size, ordinal depths, synthetic data, etc) :arrow_right: depth from SfM/stereo :arrow_right: self-supervised (minimizing the image reconstruction error)
  • challenges: object motion

Proposed Solution

  • Minimum reprojection loss, designed to robustly handle occlusions. $L_p = \min_{t^\prime} pe(I_t, I_{t^\prime \rightarrow t})$
  • Full-resolution multi-scale sampling
  • Auto-masking loss to ignore training pixels that violate camera motion assumptions. Mask $\mu = [\min_{t^\prime}pe(I_t, I_{t^\prime \rightarrow t}) < \min_{t^\prime} pe(I_t, I_{t^\prime})]$ prevents the pixels which remain stationary

Experiments(design/sufficient/datasets/open source…)

  • 超出现有算法很多（mono video， stereo， and both）
  • Ablation： 3 components + ImageNet pretraining + Training with static frames (no camera motion)

  • Other: KITTI Odometry, KITTI Depth Prediction Benchmark, Make3D

  • Open source monodepth2
